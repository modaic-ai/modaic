### Why use Modaic?

At scale, using LLMs to automate human decision-making fails in three ways:

1. Language models can't reliably flag their own edge cases
2. Manual instruction fixes are tedious, unsystematic, and risk regression
3. To measure success, you must either review everything or random sample

Modaic is the fastest way to render expert judgment into aligned, auditable LLM decisions. We introduce **Arbiters: language models that know when they don't know**. Reviewer corrections automatically compile back into better instructions.

What you can build:

- **LLM-as-a-judge** that scores agent trajectories or outputs the way your reviewers would.
- **Automated content moderation** aligned to your standards for trust and safety.
- **Routing and triaging systems** for support tickets, sales leads, content, and customer messages aligned to your team's taxonomy.
- **Semantic tagging, labeling, and quality gating** for training sets, eval datasets, and trace metadata.
- **LLM Verfier for non-verfiable RL** without risking reward hacking.
- **Fraud detection, risk scoring/underwriting, and KYC** for transactions, claims, and onboarding flows.
- And more!

## The continual learning loop created

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define a contract for your model">
    Declare your decision as structured inputs, structured outputs, and base
    instructions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run your model on unlabeled, representative data">
    As your model runs across data, it returns a decision, reasoning, and a
    confidence score.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review low-confidence cases">
    Unsure decisions cluster where the model is most likely to disagree with
    you. Filter and review those instead of the obvious examples.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Align to expert preferences">
    Modaic compiles those corrections into better instructions automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ship confidently">
    With confidence scores: new edge cases, data drift, and regressions are
    automatically detected and surfaced for review.
  </Step>
</Steps>
