### Why Modaic?

When using AI for repeated judgment tasks, it is often not enough to call a large, general-purpose model on a generic prompt and hope the output is good enough. In practice, teams need AI systems that are:

- **Aligned** with their organization's decision preferences and standards
- **Consistent** across inputs, with lower variance than ad hoc prompting workflows
- **Efficient** in both cost and time to run at scale across large datasets and recurring workflows
- **Aware of uncertainty**, so low-confidence cases can be escalated, routed, or reviewed

Modaic offers a more reliable path: **_arbiters_**.

### How do **_arbiters_** work?

[![Modaic Architecture](/images/mo-architecture.png)](/images/mo-architecture.png)

<Steps>

<Step>
  {" "}
  Start with a generic task definition such as binary classification, quality
  assessment, or safety compliance, as well as your choice of open-source model.
</Step>
<Step>
  {" "}
  As your model is run across real-world examples, Modaic extracts a calibrated
  confidence score for each prediction. Confidence scores allow you to focus
  data-labeling at the **decision boundary** - out-of-distribution examples and
  edge cases where the model is uncertain - instead of upfront bulk labeling.
</Step>
<Step>
  {" "}
  Teams can then review difficult examples, encode preferences through feedback
  and ground-truth labels, and Modaic automatically refines the system prompt
  until performance meets their standards.
</Step>
<Step>
  {" "}
  Once deployed, **_arbiters_** can continue to be monitored, re-evaluated, and
  updated as tasks, data, or decision preferences evolve.
</Step>

</Steps>

### Getting started

Modaic is built for tasks that fall within a **discrete decision space** - where the possible outcomes are known and finite. Since **_arbiters_** can be run as code or a completions endpoint, they plug seamlessly into your existing workflows, like [Braintrust](https://www.braintrust.dev/), [Langsmith](https://smith.langchain.com/), or offline evaluation.

We are currently in private beta, but feel free to reach out to us [here](https://modaic.dev/contact) to get an access code.

### FAQ

<Accordion title="How are confidence scores calculated?">
  Existing confidence-scoring methods, such as verbalized confidence and
  multi-generation methods, are often either poorly calibrated or
  computationally expensive. Modaic probes the hidden states of the model for
  fast, reliable uncertainty estimation.
</Accordion>
