### Why Modaic?

When using AI for repeated judgment tasks, it is often not enough to call a large general-purpose model on a generic prompt and hope the output is good enough. In practice, teams need systems that are:

- **Aligned** with their organization’s decision preferences and standards
- **Consistent** across inputs, with lower variance than ad hoc prompting workflows
- **Efficient** in both cost and time to run at scale across large datasets and recurring workflows
- **Aware of uncertainty**, so low-confidence cases can be escalated, routed, or reviewed

Modaic offers a more reliable path: **Arbiters**.

### How do Arbiters work?

[![Modaic Architecture](/images/mo-architecture.png)](/images/mo-architecture.png)

<Steps>

<Step>
  {" "}
  Start with a generic task definition such as binary classification, quality
  assessment, or safety compliance, as well as your choice of open-source model.
</Step>
<Step>
  {" "}
  As your model is ran across real-world examples, Modaic extracts a confidence
  score and the output from the model. Confidence scores allow you to focus
  data-labeling at the **decision boundary** — out-of-distribution examples and
  edge cases where the model is uncertain — instead of upfront bulk labeling.
  Modaic probes the hidden states of the model for reliable uncertainty
  estimation.
</Step>
<Step>
  {" "}
  Teams can then review difficult examples, encode preferences through feedback
  and a ground-truth annotation, and Modaic automatically refines the system
  prompt until performance meets their standards.
</Step>
<Step>
  {" "}
  Once deployed, arbiters can continue to be monitored, re-evaluated, and
  updated as tasks, data, or decision preferences evolve.
</Step>

</Steps>

### Getting started

Modaic is built for tasks that fall within a **discrete decision space** — where the possible outcomes are known and finite like extraction, ranking, or safety assessment. Since Arbiters can be ran as code or a completions endpoint, they plug seamlessly into your existing workflows, like [Braintrust](https://www.braintrust.dev/), [Langsmith](https://smith.langchain.com/), or offline evaluation.

We are currently in private beta, but feel free to reach out to us at team@modaic.dev to get an access code.
